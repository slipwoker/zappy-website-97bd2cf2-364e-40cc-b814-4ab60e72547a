Challenges of the Manual Method

  • ⏱️

    Time-consuming and resource wasteful
    Filling grout between tiles requires extensive time in the construction finishing process because the work is done manually on knees
  • 🦵

  • 💸

    Expensive and hard-to-find labor
    In the industry's labor shortage, a worker for manual grout filling is expensive and very difficult to find
  • 🧪

    Mess and need for acid cleaning
    The manual filling process leaves mess on site, requiring the use of acid for cleaning — which can damage the tiles

The Technological Advantage — Foundation for Growth

Groutbot's development plan is detailed down to the smallest detail. The technology we've designed is based on research and preliminary tests that prove a clear and measurable advantage over any existing solution — and this is the foundation for a high-potential investment.

Controlled Electric Flow

The electric injection mechanism provides uniform and precise pressure along the entire joint — without fatigue, without inconsistency. The amount of grout injected is precisely controlled in each pass.

Clean and Uniform Joints

The grout is injected directly into the joint with uniform depth and filling, without unnecessary excess on the tile surface. The final finish looks professional, clean and durable over time.

Reduced Worker Strain

The manual grout process requires significant physical effort and causes cumulative fatigue. Groutbot takes on the physical work, allowing the team to work faster and for longer hours without health impact.

Ready for Construction Site

The device is designed to withstand harsh field conditions — compact, easy to carry and resistant to dust and moisture. Quick transition between work areas, simple operation and easy cleaning at shift end.
